Kaleidoscope Project
(Qualitative Systems, Modeling Technique and Formalisms)

Kaleidoscope Project is a pilot research and development initiative intended to introduce notion of the Qualitative Dynamical Systems and to explore feasibility of mathematical modeling of behavior of the qualitative dynamical systems. The Qualitative Dynamical Systems, the Qualitative Dynamical Systems Behavior Mathematical Modeling Technique, and the Modeling Solutions proposed by the Kaleidoscope Project are described on the cluster of the webpages referred to in the course of description of the Project.

Page: Qualitative Dynamical Systems, Their
Behavior and Mathematical Modeling

Objective of this page is to introduce a notion of the class of Qualitative Dynamical System and to describe characteristics of the nature and the behavior of Qualitative Systems, constituting the basis for creation of mathematical models of these systems. This page identifies atomic element of the behavior of the systems and describes the ways of composition of the elements letting the model of a system to be able to reproduce various forms of behavior observed in the Qualitative Dynamical Systems being modeled. Page: Qualitative Dynamical Systems Behavior
Mathematical Modeling Technique

The page introduces Qualitative Dynamical Systems Behavior Mathematical Modeling Technique developed by the Kaleidoscope Project and describes modeling solutions proposed by the Modeling Technique. As the behavior of dynamical systems is generally perceived as a sequence of observed transitions of states of properties of the systems over time, modeling solutions suggest:

(a) defining a model as a collection of variables that represent qualitative properties of modeled system and therefore are supposed to take qualitative values;

(b) suggests mechanism of computation of changes of values of the state variables of the models in the form of state function associated with every variable; and provides the algebraic system, elements of carrier set of which could be used to represent qualitative values of the variables, while the operations defined on the set can be used for carrying out steps of computation of the functions. Qualitative Dynamical Systems Modeling
Technique's Modeling Solutions

There are three major modeling solutions based on the Algebra of Symbols, developed in the Kaleidoscope project. They are: 1) "Extended Disjunctive Normal Form (XDNF)", 2) "Modele's Vector State Space Representation", and 3) "Multi-colored Logical Net Graphical Modeling Formalism".

Solution 1: Extended Disjunctive Normal Form and
XDNF-Expression Calculator

The page, titled: "Extended Disjunctive Normal Form Expression Calculator", illustrates a special case of describing the dependence of state of one qualitative variable on the state of a group of qualitative variables. This dependence is described as a qualitative function, expression of which is defined in the form of Extended Disjunctive Normal Form (XDNF). Unlike simple DNF, XDNF includes not only familiar operations Conjunction and Disjunction which are also present in the Algebra of Symbol, but also three other operations of the Algebra called: Equivalence, Production, and Application. Meaning of these operations is described on the page. Open the Page

Solution 2: Qualitative System Model State Space Matrix
Equation and the Equation Calculator

The page, titled: "Qualitative Dynamical Systems Vector State-Space Models Simulation Calculator", illustrates mathematical modeling technique based on the representation of qualitative dynamical systems in the form of vector state equation of the systems. In simple models, the page demonstrates mechanism of transformation of state of the models in style: step of the computation - visualization of the computation result. Open the Page

Solution 3: Multi-colored Logical Net (McLN) Graphical
Modeling Formalism

The page, titled: "Qualitative Dynamical Systems Multi-Colored Logical Net Graphical Modeling Formalism", outlines the essence of the questions arising at the time of developing mathematical models of Qualitative Dynamical Systems. It also sets up requirements for the algebraic operations and justifies the necessity of having the algebraic system, operations of which could be utilized for defining mathematical models of behavior of qualitative systems.

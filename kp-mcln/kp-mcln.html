Multicolored Logical Net Graphical
Modeling Formalism

This page introduces Multicolor Logical Net Modeling Formalism, or McLN MF, which is intended for creating models of the behavior of qualitative dynamical systems in the form of their graphical models.

McLN models can be assembled, on a computer screen, using four graphical elements - properties, facts, dependence of facts on properties and the dependence of the properties on the facts. Accordingly, the McLN MF can be considered as the graphical construction set consisting of four elements. Therefore, the McLN model is, indeed, created as the graphical net, structure of which is based on the notion of bipartite directed graph, while the mechanism of reproducing behavior of the modeled system is provided by associating with the elements of the graph operations of the AoS.

Structure of the McLN Model

Being based on the notion of bipartite directed graph, the McLN model is constructed of two types of nodes, depicted as the big and small circles, and two types of arcs directed form big to small, and from small to big circles. Simple example of McLN model is presented in Figure 1.

Figure 1. One Property depending on three situations.

Big circles are the Properties. They represent properties and states of the properties of the modeled systems, which are indicated as solid color that fills the circles. Small circles are the Conditions causing changes of states of Properties. Hence, their states may either represent fact that expected situation is recognized or the fact that it is not recognized. These states of are represented as the solid white or gray colors, respectively.

Arcs directed from Property nodes to a Condition node represent dependence of the fact of recognition of situation on a certain combination of states of Property nodes. These arcs are marked with the colored dots, particular colors of which are those expected states that the arcs’ input Properties should have in order for the situation to be deemed as existing and to be recognized.

Arcs directed from a Condition node to some Property nodes represent dependence of the state of the Property nodes on the fact of existence of the expected situation. These arcs have a small arrow head marked with the color of that proposed state which the arcs' output Property nodes should take when the state of Condition node indicate fact that the expected situation is exists.

So, structure of the McLN literally mean that the state of each Condition node depends on the state of one or more of its input Property nodes, characterizing particular expected situation determined by the colors of the dots marking the Properties' output arcs. And, at the same time, the state of each Property node depends on the state of one or more of its input Condition nodes, and should take state denoted by the color marking the arrow head of the arc directed form the Condition node to the dependent Property node in case when the Condition indicates that all its input Properties represent expected situation denoted by the marks of their output arcs.

Reproducibility of Behavior of Modeled System in
McLN Model

Although pure mathematically, the bipartite directed graph is merely two sets of nodes connected by the directed arcs, the McLN model of a qualitative dynamical system, structure of which is based on the bipartite graph, in addition to the roles assigned to the elements of the graph, described above, should also be capable of reproducing behavior of modeled system. And that means the graph should have in its possession a collection of functions associated with its elements. These functions have to ensure certain transformations of states of Property nodes, and the motion of their states from some Property nodes to others. And, thus, all that activity should be performed by the Property nodes, Condition nodes and by the both types of the arcs of the graph itself. And this is achieved by endowing each element of the model with the ability to perform operation of the algebra of symbol, associated with it.

Execution of the model assumes that the states of its all properties are always defined. Accordingly, performance of the operations of the elements of the model always begins with identifying situations present in the model.

Arcs directed from Properties to Conditions are recognizers of expected states of their input Properties. Respectively, each this arc implements the Equivalence operation, which compares the color of its input Property with the color that marks the arc.

Conditions perform recognition of the fact of presence of the expected situation in the model. Therefore, each input arc of the Condition implements Conjunction operation, task of which is to compute conjunction of all the results of comparisons performed by its input arcs.

Arcs directed from Condition to Properties are generators of the proposed state of their output Properties. They implement Production operations. The task of each such arc is to produce proposed state represented by the color marking the arrowhead of the arc in the case when state of its input Condition is the fact of the existence of the expected situation. Or, alternatively, to produce proposed state represented by the color indicating that the expected situation is not recognized.

Properties accept results produced by arcs directed to them from all Conditions. Each Property should accept state computed based on all proposed states produced by all generator arcs entering it. And since the new state of Property may be not always computed, the Property should implement two operations - Disjunction and Application. Disjunction operation should compute final proposed value, and Application operation should either change the color of the property to the color computed by the Disjunction operation, when it is defined, or leaves the state of the Property unchanged when the result of the Disjunction operation turns out to be undefined.

Example of Qualitative System Model Developed Based on McLN Graphical Modeling Formalism

Presented below example is the McLN model of popular discrete dynamical system widely known as the "Dining Philosophers". States of properties of this system are considered qualitative and the mechanizm of behavior is based on Qualitative System Behavior Specification described on the page "". The McLN view of this model is presented in the figure 1.

Figure 1. Model of qualitative dynamical system: “Dining Philosophers”.

In this example the model is implemented as the McLN, and thus in the form of a bipartite directed graph. Hence, all the properties of the modeled system which are the five Philosophers and five Chopsticks, depicted in the model as the big and small circles, respectively.

According to observed behavior of the modeled system real Philosophers may be in four different stated: Contemplating, Hungry, Waiting for Availability of Chopsticks and Dining. Their behavior iterates through named four states where state Dining transitions to state Contemplating and circle repeats.

Behavior of this model is driven by two external input events where the first one causes feeling of hungriness, and the second indicates that the period of Dining is ended with satisfaction and the philosopher may proceed with strolling and contemplating again.

Both of these input events are generated by the Qualitative Dynamical System Modeling & Simulation Environment used for creating McLN models and simulation of their behavior. These input events are provided with the help of a list of tick/action pairs, called a State Driven Program. While, the program is being created and associated with a property whose state can change as a result of external input-events, during constructing of the model.
